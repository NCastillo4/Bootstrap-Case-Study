Case Study

Project Overview

The Problem: Users currently aren’t connecting with Habitat for Humanity’s website due to an inundation of information that is unclear and unorganized. Users that visit the website in search of volunteer opportunities in particular find that the navigation flow to get to the correct information is confusing.

The Solution: Redesign and optimize the website to create an engaging and productive experience for the users, so that they are more inclined to volunteer and reach their goals as well as helping their community.

User Research

We took a hybrid approach to our initial user research interviews by conducting a combination of task testing of the existing website as well as asking users interview questions. This helped us gain a better understanding of each user’s familiarity with the website and organization prior to the user test. This approach also helped us to learn what each user liked and disliked regarding the existing website’s usability, flow and engagement

Affinity Diagram

Key Insights:

  • Users aren’t connecting (emotionally) with the website
  • Navigation menu is confusing due to verbage and an abundance of secondary navigation categories is overwhelming
  • There’s a lot of content available on the website, it just needs to be refined and better organized
  • It’s confusing to get to volunteer options and/or sign up page which can be frustrating/discouraging for the user

User Persona

Prioritization Matrix

Our Prioritization Matrix helped us narrow down main features we would incorporate into the redesign which we were able to include most of, such as; filtering/ selecting volunteer options based on availability, project description, clearer text, connection

Ideas such as communicating with other users on the same platform would become a feature development item

Card Sorting

During user testing many users found the navigation menu to be confusing due to the wording used as well as an overload of subcategories in the secondary navigation.

Our goal was to simplify and make the main navigation more relatable, intuitive and also help form a connection with the organization by using headings such as “ Who We Are” What We Do”, etc.

Paper Prototypes

Although each of our approaches was different, we liked the varying aspects we hadn’t thought of and worked on incorporating details from each paper prototype (such as icons, cards, location details, etc.)

Lo-Fi Testing

  • Users found the “Our Goal” and we “Need Your Support” sections on the homepage confusing. This helped us decide to eliminate “Our Goal” and display the mission at the forefront of the layout and reworded “ We Need Your Support” to “Other Ways To Give”.
  • The Accessibility, Help & Contact Us items were too visually similar in the navigation menu. We thought it would then be best to change these to “icons with text” to differentiate them from the menu items
  • We learned that planning was more important to users vs. demographics in the filters which led to our decision to move planning categories to the top of the filters
  • Users liked the option of being able to apply or save on the “Project Page”, however, the “Save” button was a bit confusing as they were unclear what they were saving. We decided to incorporate a heart icon on the project page so the user could click the heart to save to their “Saved Projects”

Future Development

  • Filter and organize information in other parts of the website that are essential. In particular, the Donation page as there are so many ways to donate that it may be helpful to simplify, condense and display that information in way that doesn’t feel overwhelming for users.
  • Create a messaging platform within the site. For instance, if a volunteer wants to direct message regarding a particular project or connecting with other volunteers.
  • Incorporate notes and calendar section within a users profile so they may keep their notes, project dates, etc within their profile.
